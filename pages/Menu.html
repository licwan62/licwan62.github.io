Experience a traditional Sri Lankan meal catered by Rasai and taste the complexity and the richness of Sri Lankan culture. Rasia believes in the authentic preparation and freshness of Sri Lankan food, and source many of the ingredients from farmer’s markets, our own vegetable garden, specifically stores and local butchers.

Abulthiyal (fish ambul thiyal)

Ambulthiyal, also known as malu ambulthiyal is a must-try Sri Lankan sour fish curry. Made with Goraka (dried garcinia) and black pepper, not only gives the fish ambulthiyal (sour fish) curry a unique taste but is also a method of preserving fish without refrigeration or freezing.

Kukul mas curry (chicken curry)

Kukul mas is a Colombo-style tradition Sri Lankan chicken curry. This creamy and lightly spiced curry is typically made with diced chicken breast, onions, ginger, garlic, turmeric, garam masala, chilli powder, coconut milk, tomatoes, oil, and salt.

Parippu (dhal curry)

Parippu Curry is basically a Moong Lentil Curry, tempered in a typical South Indian style. It has a good dose of coconut, just like any other dish in this part of our country, and other spices.

Polos (green Jackfruit curry)

Polos curry or polos ambula is a traditional Sri Lankan baby jackfruit curry. This jackfruit curry is made using jackfruit which has not matured or ripped, at this stage of the jackfruit, the fruit is often referred to as baby jackfruit or green jackfruit.

Wambatu moju (eggplant/brinjals pickle)

Wambatu moju aka brinjal moju are fried eggplant strips, shallots, and green chillies mixed with mustard vinegar to pickle them, giving it a combo of sweet, sour, and heat.

Gotu kola sambol (pennywort salad)

A green salad with chopped Gotu kola and freshly scraped coconut gives you one of the healthiest salads in Sri Lankan cuisine.

Kottu (also kottu roti)

Kottu translates from Sinhalese as chopped, and this hugely popular Sri Lankan street food consists of chopped up roti (a type of flatbread), stir-fried with chicken, vegetables and egg.

Lamprais

Lamprais is a Sri Lankan meal, a marriage of multiple elements – samba rice, brinjal and meat curries, seeni sambol, fried ash plantain and blachan – steamed and packed in a banana leaf. Lamprais recipes have typically been a burgher family tradition. It is a meal with a strong, yet gradually wavering legacy.

Hoppers (appa or appam)

An appa or appam is a type of thin pancake originating from South India and Sri Lanka, It is made with fermented rice batter and coconut milk, traditionally cooked in an appachatti, a deep pan similar in shape to a wok.

Pol Sambol (coconut relish)

It is a coconut relish, consisting of fresh grated coconut, shallots, dried whole chillies or chili powder, lime juice, and salt.
